UCSD Parking Mockumentary

A comedic, half-fiction half-truth documentary about the state of student parking at UCSD.

Overview

Based on the experience of one of our team members, this mockumentary highlights the dearth of parking spaces for both students and professors at UC San Diego.

It begins with real interviews with students who park at Gliderport, a city-owned dirt parking lot near the north end of campus that has free parking during the day. We then follow Hannah as she spends her morning searching for student parking spaces at the Pangea and Hopkins parking structures, only to find herself having to go to Gliderport. Professor Patty Ahn then details their experience with parking as a faculty member, stating that the admin have acknowledged the issue but cannot help. This is immediately refuted by "Chancellor Khosla", who approves a message saying that there is no parking problem on campus.

We wanted to use our filmmaking skills to create something that was fun but also highlighted real issues we faced as students.

During the shooting process, I was primarily involved in deciding the type of footage needed and organizing our schedule. When it came to editing, my team members commended me for my strong grasp of what shots we had and for my ability to determine where certain footage would have the most impact. I also helped with setting up filming and audio recording equipment, as well as gathering foley sound effects.
