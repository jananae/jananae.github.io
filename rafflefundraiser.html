SGA Raffle Fundraiser

Social media graphics and other assets for an archery club fundraiser

Overview

As the Director of External Affairs during my last year as an officer for Sun God Archery, I was in charge of planning and overseeing all club fundraisers, hosting at least one per quarter. In spring of that year, with a new lockdown order in place, we wouldn't be able to rely on our usual event-based fundraisers. After discussion with the rest of the board, we decided on a 50/50 raffle fundraiser. Half the raffle ticket sales would go towards the club, while the rest would be given out in the form of archery equipment or gift card prizes. Ticket sales ran from late March to mid-April. The prize drawing was held over Zoom on April 17, followed by a film viewing planned by our social chair.

I was responsible for all aspects of the fundraiser, from marketing to choosing prizes to determining prize tier cut-offs, as well as making all necessary materials. I posted regular updates on how close the next tier was to being unlocked on the club's Facebook page and Discord channels. On Instagram, I made an initial post to our main profile, then followed up with Instagram Story updates as new tiers were unlocked.

With a raffle-based fundraiser, we would be able to operate throughout lockdown. Opting to include both archery equipment and cash prizes allowed us to continue helping our members gain access to equipment they might otherwise not be able to afford, while also incentivizing non-archers to support our club.


Prizes


Prizes were chosen after careful consideration of what members were likely to not have, as well as what items often needed replacing. As our club welcomes archers of multiple shooting styles, all of which have different equipment requirements, at least two prizes per tier were chosen to be useful to any archer. Specific brands were chosen for each object after consultation with team coaches and reference to the existing club equipment recommendations.


Social Media Graphics



Graphics were optimized for Instagram, as it had the strictest limitations on image dimensions. The initial post featured the first image, a single announcement with a bit.ly link for more info that was also included in the image caption.

A looping video version of the gif started off our story, with updates at the end of second and third weeks. Tiers were represented with fire icons to evoke phrases like the colloquial "on fire" and AAVE/slang term "lit". Story images were accompanied by informally placed text in the empty spaces above and below, encouraging users to swipe up for the bit.ly link to learn more and buy tickets.

Results

In the end we had 21 people participate in the raffle drawing, selling over 100 tickets total. Out of the three winners, two were club members, while the third was a friend of someone in our community. Two winners chose cash prizes, while the third chose the quiver from the 4th tier. Prizes were distributed by mail and outdoors in person.

We raised over $200 for the club, on par with some of our most popular past fundraisers. Considering the context of this fundraiser replacing an in-person social fundraising event, the proceeds were higher than we had expected.

With venues opening again, the club will be returning to in-person fundraisers, as they serve the additional function of being a social event. However, the success of our raffle fundraiser has definitely made it a strong contender for a yearly tradition.
