CalGames 2017 Program Book

A program book for an off-season robotics competition hosted by WRRF

Overview

CalGames is an annual high school robotics competition hosted by the Western Region Robotics Forum (WRRF), a non-profit organization focused on supporting STEM education. CalGames occurs during the off-season of the FIRST Robotics Competitions (FRC) program, using modified rules of that year's FRC season. Having attended and aided previous WRRF events in the past, I was asked by a mentor to help produce media for the 2017 game. Due to scheduling conflicts he was unable to help out that year, and recruited me and a few others to take over his role.

After a series of meetings, we determined that I would be responsible for creating the 2017 CalGames program book, basing it on the layouts of previous years. I was given the InDesign file from the previous year for reference, as well as existing graphical assets such as organization logos, but was otherwise allowed creative freedom over the program book layout.

Challenges

Creating the program book was, in some ways, more difficult than I'd expected. For one, the game happening during the off-season meant that all the planning coincided with my first month as a college student. I was balancing five classes in UCSD's quarter system, joining a new college club, and working on the program book layout. To top it all off, this was my first experience using InDesign, so I was also teaching myself to use the program. Additionally, much of the information that needed to go in the program book was missing, especially as volunteers and plans changed leading up the deadline.

Luckily, I had support from the other volunteers as we all worked to ensure a quality event. I spent my time between classes learning InDesign and gathering the necessary information, leaving placeholders in spaces where the information wasn't available yet. After every significant change, I sent updates to the event organizers, noting what edits I'd made and what I still needed to do. With their feedback, I iterated a total of five versions, finalizing the layout and information in time for it to be printed.

Results

I myself was unable to attend CalGames due to being in San Diego for college, but heard afterwards that the event had gone surprisingly well given all the hiccups that occurred during the planning stages. The program book was well received, and my mentor approved of it as well, noting that it "looked a tad cleaner than [their] version last year". In the end though, I was asked to make one last version to put online, as even after printing we had unexpected informational changes. Still, the event organizers and I were pleased with the final outcome of the program book design.
