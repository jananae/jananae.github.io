Homemade Merlot Wine Lablels

A personal design project for a homemade wine

Overview

For a number of years now, my parents have been interested in homesteading. It started with vegetable gardening, then owning chickens, so when a family friend offered us a bucket of home-grown grapes for making wine, they gratefully accepted. They had made passionfruit wine in the past and created their own label for those bottles, but wanted me to design the label for this batch of grape wine. I was given the label for the friend's own wine as informational reference and size requirements, then left to my own devices to work on the design.


Challenges

Truthfully, I had assumed that with the little information I'd been given, I had complete creative freedom. The first designs I came up with were based on my personal preferences in what I'd seen on other wine bottles, with a clean and modern look.


The grape version came about after my parents' feedback that image on the first felt like it didn't say anything about the contents being wine, as well as the information on the reference label being incorrect for our usage. However, even after the edits, they weren't quite satisfied with the design. They asked for me to make a horizontal label instead, and to remove all of what they deemed unnecessary or incorrect informationâ€”things such as the name, ABV percentage, and the source of the grapes.


While these were better received, my parents felt that the simplistic style didn't quite fit what they wanted. Confused about what exactly they were looking for, I asked if they had any references for what style they liked. They showed me the label for the passionfruit wine they had generated, which gave me a much better understanding of the more traditional aesthetic they preferred. With that new knowledge, I was able to come up with a design they approved of.


Results

After the approval of my final design, I sent my parents a PNG file for them to print onto labels. They came out quite very clean when printed, and made for nice packaging when gifting the bottles to friends.
